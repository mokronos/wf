Employee Offboarding and Access Revocation

Business workflow for scheduled, immediate, and sensitive offboarding across HR, IT, legal, and finance systems.

What It Should Do

Coordinate a departing employee's account deactivation, device recovery, data transfer, payroll tasks, legal holds, and manager handoff. The workflow must handle both planned departures and urgent involuntary terminations with strict timing.

Programs and Services Involved

People Systems
HRIS, payroll, benefits, background check notes, manager approval.
Access Systems
Identity provider, MDM, password manager, Git hosting, cloud consoles, VPN, SaaS admin APIs.
Operations
Asset inventory, shipping provider, legal hold system, shared drive ownership transfer, ticket tracker.

Workflow Diagram

flowchart TD
  A[HR creates offboarding event] --> B{Departure type}
  B -- planned --> C[Schedule tasks relative to last day]
  B -- immediate --> D[Start emergency revocation timer]
  B -- sensitive --> E[Restrict visibility and notify legal]
  C --> F[Manager selects data owners and handoff plan]
  D --> G[Disable SSO, VPN, and privileged access]
  E --> G
  F --> H[Prepare access revocation batch]
  H --> I[Last-day trigger fires]
  I --> G
  G --> J[Revoke SaaS and infrastructure access in parallel]
  J --> K[Lock or wipe managed devices]
  K --> L[Transfer files, calendars, repos, and tickets]
  L --> M{Assets returned?}
  M -- no --> N[Create shipping and reminder loop]
  N --> M
  M -- yes --> O[Close payroll, benefits, and compliance tasks]
  O --> P[Generate final audit report]

Important Branches and Edge Cases

  • Employee owns production resources and revocation must wait until ownership transfer is confirmed.
  • Legal hold prevents deletion or wipe of specific data while still requiring account lockout.
  • Third-party SaaS API rate limits require batched revocation with progress tracking.
  • Manager is unavailable, so data ownership defaults to department head after a deadline.
  • Device is offline and the wipe command must remain pending until the device checks in.
  • Immediate termination requires most access revoked within minutes while noncritical cleanup continues for days.
