Research Briefing Pipeline

Knowledge workflow for collecting sources, extracting claims, reviewing summaries, and publishing a briefing.

What It Should Do

Build a daily or weekly briefing from many sources, track citations, remove duplicate stories, summarize with reviewer approval, publish to multiple destinations, and preserve the source bundle for later audit.

Programs and Services Involved

Inputs
RSS feeds, email newsletters, PDFs, web pages, shared folders, internal notes.
Processing
Browser fetcher, OCR, PDF parser, LLM summarizer, embedding index, citation validator.
Outputs
Notion or Confluence, Markdown repo, email campaign tool, Slack channel, archive bucket.

Workflow Diagram

flowchart TD
  A[Scheduled briefing window opens] --> B[Collect source candidates]
  B --> C[Normalize HTML, PDF, email, and note formats]
  C --> D[Extract title, author, date, entities, and claims]
  D --> E[Cluster duplicate and related items]
  E --> F{Enough high-quality sources?}
  F -- no --> G[Ask analyst to add sources or skip topic]
  G --> E
  F -- yes --> H[Draft topic summaries with citations]
  H --> I[Run citation and quote checks]
  I --> J{Reviewer approves?}
  J -- changes requested --> K[Return section to drafting queue]
  K --> H
  J -- approved --> L[Assemble final briefing]
  L --> M[Publish to docs, email, and chat]
  M --> N[Archive source bundle and decision log]

Important Branches and Edge Cases

  • Source date is missing or ambiguous, requiring confidence metadata and human review.
  • Two sources disagree on a key fact, so the briefing must mark the conflict instead of hiding it.
  • Paywalled sources fail to fetch automatically and require browser-assisted capture.
  • Reviewer approves some sections while sending others back for revision.
  • Publishing to one destination fails, but the workflow should not duplicate already published destinations on retry.
  • A correction arrives after publication and must update all destinations with a version trail.
