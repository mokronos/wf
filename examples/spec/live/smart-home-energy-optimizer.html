Smart Home Energy Optimizer

Local and cloud workflow for home energy scheduling across solar, battery, EV charging, HVAC, and dynamic pricing.

What It Should Do

Forecast household energy production and demand, choose an operating plan, control devices safely, respect manual overrides, and explain why actions were taken. It should continue in degraded mode when cloud services are unavailable.

Programs and Services Involved

Devices
Home Assistant, smart meter, solar inverter, battery, EV charger, thermostat, smart plugs.
Data
Weather forecast, electricity tariff API, occupancy sensors, calendar, historical consumption database.
Controls
Local MQTT, device vendor APIs, notification app, manual override dashboard, safety limits.

Workflow Diagram

flowchart TD
  A[Every 15 minutes or sensor event] --> B[Read meter, solar, battery, EV, HVAC, and occupancy state]
  B --> C[Fetch weather and price forecast]
  C --> D{Cloud data available?}
  D -- no --> E[Use cached forecast and conservative rules]
  D -- yes --> F[Build optimized energy plan]
  E --> G[Validate plan against safety and comfort constraints]
  F --> G
  G --> H{Manual override active?}
  H -- yes --> I[Respect override and record skipped actions]
  H -- no --> J[Dispatch device commands]
  J --> K[Verify device state changes]
  K --> L{Command failed?}
  L -- yes --> M[Retry local path, then notify user]
  M --> N[Update plan state]
  L -- no --> N
  I --> N
  N --> O[Log explanation and projected savings]

Important Branches and Edge Cases

  • EV must be charged by a user-defined departure time even if electricity prices remain high.
  • Battery reserve must be preserved during storm warnings or grid outage risk.
  • Device command succeeds through local MQTT but vendor cloud state lags behind, requiring reconciliation.
  • Occupancy sensors are contradictory, so HVAC comfort rules take precedence over savings.
  • Dynamic tariff API is down, so the workflow must fall back to cached rates and avoid risky arbitrage.
  • User manually turns on a device and the workflow must avoid fighting the override for a configured window.
