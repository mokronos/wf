Vendor Onboarding and Procurement

Business workflow covering intake, risk checks, approvals, contract routing, ERP setup, and purchase order creation.

What It Should Do

Turn an employee's request for a new vendor into a compliant, payable supplier record and an approved purchase order. The workflow should coordinate humans, external systems, document generation, parallel approvals, retries, and auditable decisions.

Programs and Services Involved

Internal
Slack or Teams, Jira Service Management, ERP, contract repository, identity provider.
External
SOC 2 portal, sanctions screening API, tax form validation, e-signature service, banking verification provider.
Artifacts
Vendor intake form, NDA, DPA, W-9 or W-8, purchase order, audit log, risk scorecard.

Workflow Diagram

flowchart TD
  A[Employee submits vendor request] --> B{Duplicate vendor?}
  B -- yes --> C[Link requester to existing vendor record]
  B -- no --> D[Collect vendor profile and documents]
  D --> E[Run sanctions, tax, bank, and domain checks]
  E --> F{Risk level}
  F -- low --> G[Manager approval]
  F -- medium --> H[Manager and finance approvals in parallel]
  F -- high --> I[Security, legal, finance, and exec approvals]
  G --> J[Generate contract packet]
  H --> J
  I --> J
  J --> K[Route for e-signature]
  K --> L{Signed before deadline?}
  L -- no --> M[Escalate and remind]
  M --> K
  L -- yes --> N[Create supplier in ERP]
  N --> O[Create purchase order]
  O --> P[Notify requester and vendor owner]

Important Branches and Edge Cases

  • Request is a duplicate but the existing vendor lacks a required compliance document.
  • Vendor domain does not match banking information, requiring manual finance review.
  • Approver is out of office, so approval must delegate after a timeout.
  • Legal redlines the DPA and sends the workflow into a negotiation loop with versioned documents.
  • ERP supplier creation succeeds but purchase order creation fails and must be retried idempotently.
  • Requester cancels after approvals but before contract signature, requiring cancellation notices and audit closure.
